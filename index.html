Kimono

The kimono is a traditional Japanese garment and the national dress of Japan. The kimono is a T-shaped, wrapped-front garment with square sleeves and a rectangular body, and is worn left side wrapped over right, unless the wearer is deceased.

Hikizuri (引きずり)

Hikizuri kimono was worn by wealthy women of high rank.

Tomesode (留袖)

Tomesode is the most formal kimono type worn by married women.

Houmongi (訪問着)

Houmongi is “visiting wear”, and both married and unmarried women wear these semi-formal kimono types.

Iro Muji (色無地)

These kimonos types have a plain color without any patterns.
